  • ✓

  • ✓
    Cracked/broken tiles or displaced ridge caps

What’s Included in a Tile Leak Repair

Targeted leak repair scoped to the affected area. We address the water path—not just the surface tiles.

  • ✓
    Leak source tracing in the suspected area (valleys, headwalls, penetrations, dead valleys, transitions).
  • ✓
    Tile lift & stage (carefully remove and set aside tiles to access underlayment).
  • ✓
    Remove failed underlayment and install new underlayment (two-ply felt or synthetic per site conditions).
  • ✓
    Replace/augment flashing (headwall, sidewall, pan/valley metal, pipe flashing as required).
  • ✓
    Re-set tiles with appropriate fasteners; replace broken tiles with matching type when available.
  • ✓
    Seal and paint exposed fasteners and metal as needed; clear valleys and restore drainage.

If matching tiles are discontinued, we’ll blend best-match stock from inventory or local suppliers and note any visible variation.

Our 4-Step Repair Process

Simple, methodical, and photo-documented.

1) Source the Leak
Moisture tracking, test areas, and inspection around valleys, walls, and penetrations.
2) Lift Tiles
Carefully remove and stage tiles to access underlayment and flashing.
3) New Underlayment & Flashing
Replace failed underlayment; upgrade/repair valley and penetration flashing.
4) Reset & Seal
Re-install tiles, fasten, seal, paint touch-ups, and clean the work area.

Tile Repair FAQ

Do you work on both concrete and clay tile?
Yes. We service concrete and clay systems. Some clay tiles are fragile or discontinued; we’ll advise on best-match replacements and handling.
Will a localized repair stop the leak for good?
If the water path is limited to a specific area (e.g., valley, penetration, headwall), a localized repair is effective. Widespread underlayment wear may require a larger lift-and-lay scope.
Do you provide photos?
Yes—before, during, and after photos with notes on what we found and fixed.
