What We Do on a Tile Roof Repair

Targeted repairs to stop active leaks and reinforce vulnerable transitions.

  • ✓
    Leak tracing (visual, moisture check, and water test as needed) to confirm the repair area.
  • ✓
    Careful removal & staging of tiles in the suspect area; replace broken tiles with close match when available.
  • ✓
    New underlayment in the repair zone (tie into sound upslope material); replace damaged battens as needed.
  • ✓
    Flashing corrections at walls, headwalls, skylights, or penetrations (step/headwall/apron or pipe flashings as required).
  • ✓
    Reinstall tiles, set and secure; seal fasteners and touch up exposed metal to help prevent rust.
  • ✓
    Clean-up & photo notes so you can see what we found and fixed.

We reuse your existing tiles whenever possible. Color variation can occur when replacement tiles are sourced.

Our Process

Proven steps for fast, durable tile roof repairs.

1) Diagnose
Trace the leak source; define the working area.
2) Lift & Stage
Remove tiles carefully; set aside safely.
3) Underlayment
Replace aged/damaged felt or synthetic in the zone.
4) Flashing
Install or correct step/headwall/pipe flashings.
5) Reset Tiles
Reinstall, replace broken pieces, seal fasteners.
6) Verify & Report
Water-test when appropriate; provide photos.

Scope & Limits

Included (Typical)

  • Leak tracing and targeted demo of the affected area
  • New underlayment in repair zone (tie-in to sound material)
  • New or corrected flashing at the leak source
  • Tile reset and minor replacements (match when available)
  • Sealants at necessary transitions and fasteners

May Require Additional Work

  • Large areas of brittle underlayment or widespread failures
  • Extensive wood replacement (decking, fascia, rafter tails)
  • Custom metals, skylight replacements, or new penetrations
  • Access/safety for steep or two-story roofs

Many tile roofs need full lift-and-relay (new underlayment throughout) once the underlayment approaches ~25–30 years. We’ll advise if a localized repair won’t be cost-effective long-term.

FAQ

Will this repair stop my leak for good?
If the leak source is within the repair zone and the surrounding underlayment is sound, yes. If the roof is aging broadly, a full lift-and-relay may be the better fix. We’ll document our findings and recommendations.
Do you work with concrete and clay tiles?
Yes. We reuse your existing tiles when possible. Clay can be fragile on older roofs; we’ll replace only what’s necessary and source close matches when available.
What underlayment do you use for repairs?
Premium felt or synthetic underlayment rated for tile systems. We tie into sound material upslope and correct flashings at the leak source.
