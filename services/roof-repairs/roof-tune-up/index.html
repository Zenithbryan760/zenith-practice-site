Is a tune-up right for your roof?

Great candidates typically include:

  • Roofs under ~30 years with localized wear
  • Roofs with minor leaks at pipes/penetrations
  • Valleys/gutters collecting debris and slowing drainage

Not ideal when: underlayment is at end-of-life (common near 30 years on tile), there’s widespread shingle granule loss, saturated decking, or systemic flashing failure—in those cases, replacement is the smarter investment.

Why a tune-up helps

Small maintenance now can buy time and prevent nuisance leaks, especially around penetrations and drainage paths.

Penetration sealing

Pipes and roof jacks are the most common leak points. We reseal, replace brittle boots, and secure flashings.

Valleys & gutters

We clear debris so water can shed properly—simple, effective, and often overlooked.

Loose/missing pieces

Reset slipped tiles/shingles and replace missing ones to close easy entry points for water.

Common questions

How long does a tune-up last?

It’s maintenance, not a new roof. The goal is to slow wear, fix small issues, and buy time. On a healthy sub-structure, the benefit can be meaningful; on roofs at the end of life, it’s a short-term patch.

Do you guarantee no future leaks?

We warrant our workmanship on the specific areas we service. A tune-up can’t reverse age-related failure (e.g., brittle underlayment) or defects in areas we didn’t touch.

Will you replace a lot of broken tiles/shingles?

We replace missing/damaged pieces we can reasonably match during the visit. If a roof needs broader material replacement, we’ll recommend a targeted repair or full replacement.

Friendly fine print

  • Tune-ups are intended for maintainable roofs, generally under ~30 years.
  • Interior damage, unrelated leaks, or issues caused by other trades/third parties aren’t covered.
  • If conditions indicate end-of-life (e.g., aged tile underlayment, extensive granule loss, saturated wood), we’ll recommend replacement.
