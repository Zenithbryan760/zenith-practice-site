What we do in a standard tune‑up

Every home is a little different, but these are the typical items we address during a tune‑up for single‑family roofs.

Need something more involved (large areas of damage, new underlayment, or new flashing)? We’re happy to provide a separate repair or replacement estimate.

  • Seal vent pipes on single‑family homes (pipe flashings, storm collars, and tops).
  • Caulk around roof penetrations (plumbing vents, HVAC/conduit, satellite holes) where sealant has aged or cracked.
  • Clean valleys and gutters of roof‑related debris to promote proper drainage.
  • Fix broken or slipped tiles (minor spot repairs) and secure loose pieces where feasible.
  • Touch up exposed flashings (nail heads, small sealant renewals) at common leak points.
  • General roof check with notes on anything that may need future attention.

When is a tune‑up a good idea?

  • You haven’t had the roof looked at in a few years.
  • You’ve noticed debris collecting in valleys or gutters.
  • After windy or rainy seasons that may have shifted tiles or dried old sealant.
  • Before listing a home or after moving into a home with an older roof.
