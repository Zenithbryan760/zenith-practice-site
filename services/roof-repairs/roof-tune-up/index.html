What’s Included in a Roof Tune‑Up

Scope varies by roof type and condition — we focus on leak prevention and manufacturer‑approved maintenance for tile, shingle, and flat systems.

Tile Roof Tune‑Up

  • Replace slipped or broken tiles (owner‑provided or matching stock when available)
  • Clear valleys and debris dams; improve drainage
  • Seal and fasten around penetrations (vents, pipes, satellite holes)
  • Inspect underlayment exposure; note brittle or aged felt

Shingle Roof Tune‑Up

  • Reseal lifted tabs; replace missing shingles where feasible
  • Re‑nail or screw exposed fasteners; seal heads
  • Check flashing at walls, chimneys, skylights; apply sealant where needed
  • Clean gutters & downspouts; remove roof debris

Flat Roof Tune‑Up (TPO/Mod‑Bit)

  • Inspect seams & terminations; hand‑roll and patch minor splits
  • Seal penetration boots and pitch pans
  • Clear scuppers & drains; restore proper flow
  • Note ponding areas or UV wear for future repair
Deliverables: Photo notes, maintenance recommendations, and a written report with any repairs beyond tune‑up quoted separately.

Roof Tune‑Up FAQs

What is a roof tune‑up vs. a full repair?
A tune‑up is preventative maintenance: cleaning, sealing, tightening, and small part swaps. If we find damage needing new flashing, underlayment, or larger work, we’ll quote that separately.
How often should I schedule roof maintenance?
Most homes benefit from an annual tune‑up; homes under trees, near the coast, or with older roofs may need maintenance every 6–12 months.
Will a tune‑up stop my leak?
If your leak is caused by a minor issue (like a failed sealant or debris dam), a tune‑up may resolve it. If the cause is worn underlayment or flashing failure, we’ll recommend a repair.
Do you service tile, shingle, and flat roofs?
Yes — concrete/clay tile, asphalt shingles, torch‑down, and TPO. We tailor maintenance to the roof system and age.
Do you provide pictures and recommendations?
Yes. You’ll receive photo notes and a written summary with next steps so you can plan repairs or future maintenance.
