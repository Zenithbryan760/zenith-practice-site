Tune-Up Checklist (All Roof Types)

  • ✓ Seal/replace pipe collars, vents & boots
  • ✓ Reseal flashings at walls, skylights & chimneys
  • ✓ Clean & flush gutters/downspouts
  • ✓ Clear valleys & remove roof debris
  • ✓ Seal drains & scuppers
  • ✓ Re-adhere/replace loose shingles/tiles
  • ✓ Granule restoration on worn shingle areas
  • ✓ Tighten/replace metal fasteners with new washers
  • ✓ Photo report & prioritized recommendations

Pro Tip: A tune-up every 12–24 months prevents small issues from becoming leaks—especially before winter rains or after Santa Ana winds.

Our Roof Tune-Up Process

1) Inspect

Interview, leak history, targeted inspection of planes, flashings, penetrations & drainage.

2) Prep

Debris removal, valley clean-out, access protection and safety setup.

3) Seal

Reseal flashings, collars, drains & scuppers; minor resets; fastener replacement.

4) Restore

Granule touch-ups on shingles; spot recaps on mod-bit/silicone as appropriate.

5) Report

Photo documentation with recommendations and clear next-step options.

Roof Tune-Up FAQs

How often should I get a roof tune-up?
Typically every 12–24 months, or after major wind/rain events. Commercial roofs with drains benefit from bi-annual service.
Will a tune-up fix active leaks?
Tune-ups are preventative. If we discover an active leak, we’ll perform a targeted repair (with your approval) or schedule it promptly.
Does this extend the life of an older roof?
Yes, within reason. For systems near ~25+ years in SoCal, we’ll tell you when replacement becomes the smarter spend.
Residential and commercial?
Yes—single-family homes, HOAs, retail/office and light industrial. We tailor visit frequency and reporting to your property.
