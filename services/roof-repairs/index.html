Our Repair Process

  1. Conversation & leak history: when it started, previous attempts, attic access if available.
  2. Targeted inspection: roof surface, flashings, penetrations, valleys, transitions, and nearby slope changes.
  3. Diagnosis with photos: you get a photo set with notes so you see what we see.
  4. Clear scope & price: itemized by task and materials; we flag any risks or unknowns up-front.
  5. Repair day: we protect landscaping, perform the scope, and seal/weld/fasten per manufacturer specs.
  6. Water test (as needed): controlled hose test to confirm the leak stop when conditions allow.
  7. Wrap-up & warranty: photos of completed work and written warranty for the repair area.

Rule of thumb in Southern California: once a roof system is ~25+ years old (or tile underlayment is past its life), larger repairs often cost more over time than a planned replacement. We’ll always tell you when a replacement is the smarter spend.

Common Homeowner Situations

  • Wind-lifted shingles after Santa Ana winds.
  • Slipped/cracked tiles and aging felt underlayment causing hidden leaks.
  • Flat roof ponding and open seams at TPO/PVC laps.
  • Rusty valley metal or improperly sealed penetrations.
  • Built-up blisters and alligatoring from UV exposure.
  • Metal fastener back-out and failed neoprene washers.
  • SPF foam cracks or worn topcoat exposing foam.

Asphalt Shingle Roof Repairs

Architectural & 3-tab shingles

Typical Repairs

  • Replace wind-damaged or missing shingles; seal tabs.
  • Reflash or reseal pipe jacks, skylights, chimneys.
  • Repair step/roof-to-wall flashing and cricket transitions.
  • Address nail pops, exposed fasteners, and ridge vent leaks.

When Repair Makes Sense

  • Isolated storm damage on an otherwise healthy roof (< 15–20 yrs).
  • Localized leaks around one penetration or transition.

Consider Replacement

  • Widespread granule loss, brittle shingles, or frequent blow-offs.
  • Multiple leaks across different areas.
  • System age approaching ~25+ years in SoCal conditions.

Temporary fix: asphalt mastic and replacement shingles can stop water now; plan a larger repair when weather clears.

Concrete/Clay Tile Roof Repairs

Lift & relay tiles, underlayment repairs

Typical Repairs

  • Replace cracked/slipped tiles; reset with proper headlap.
  • Open and repair valleys; replace rusted or undersized valley metal.
  • Localized underlayment replacement where leaks occur.
  • Reflash roof-to-wall, headwall, skylight, and chimney areas.

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Ceiling stain near a valley after a heavy rain.
  • Leak at a second-story wall where the patio cover meets the roof.

Repair vs. Replace

  • Tiles often outlast the underlayment; if the felt is aged/brittle widely, a lift & lay is smarter than chasing leaks.
  • For small areas, localized underlayment repair is fine; for recurring leaks, plan for full underlayment replacement.

Temporary fix: emergency tile reset and valley clean-out to stop water until we perform underlayment work.

TPO / PVC Flat Roof Repairs

Heat-welded membranes

Typical Repairs

  • Weld patches over open seams, fishmouths, or punctures.
  • Reflash pipes, HVAC stands, drains, and curb units.
  • Add walkway pads; adjust scuppers/drains for ponding areas.

When Repair Works

  • Membrane is in decent condition with isolated seam failures.
  • Penetration leaks from aged flashings.

Consider Replacement

  • Extensive UV chalking, brittleness, or widespread seam failure.
  • Water trapped under the membrane (wet insulation).
  • System age near ~20–25+ years depending on exposure.

Temporary fix: membrane-compatible tape and targeted heat-welded patches can bridge a storm; schedule permanent repairs in dry weather.

Built-Up (BUR) & Modified Bitumen Repairs

Hot-mop, cap sheet, torch-down

Typical Repairs

  • Cut and patch blisters; reinforce with modified bitumen.
  • Seal alligatoring and splits; recoat as needed.
  • Reflash drains, scuppers, and metal edge transitions.

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Ceiling spots after hot days followed by a rain (blister popped).
  • Leak at the low point near a drain or scupper.

Repair vs. Replace

  • Repairs are reliable if the plies are still sound and dry.
  • Widespread alligatoring/cracking or trapped moisture suggests new system.

Temporary fix: cold-applied mastic with polyester reinforcement to stop active drips until full repairs.

Silicone-Coated Roof Repairs

Coating touch-ups & re-seals

Typical Repairs

  • Re-seal penetrations, seams, and terminations with silicone.
  • Patch worn-through or thin areas; address ponding spots.
  • Prep & recoat aged/chalked surfaces per manufacturer spec.

When Repair Works

  • Coating is mostly intact; substrate below is dry and sound.
  • Isolated mechanical damage or traffic wear.

Consider Replacement

  • Coating failure exposing the original membrane or foam widely.
  • Poor adhesion from improper prep; saturated substrate.

Temporary fix: clean/dry and apply silicone-compatible patch to bridge a rainy week.

Metal Roof Repairs

Standing seam & corrugated roofs

Typical Repairs

  • Tighten/replace fasteners and neoprene washers; add stitch screws.
  • Seal end-laps and side-laps with butyl tape + compatible sealants.
  • Reflash penetrations and curbs; replace failed boots.

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Drip during wind-driven rain along panel laps.
  • Rust staining at fastener rows or edges.

Repair vs. Replace

  • Good candidates: limited corrosion, isolated fastener back-out.
  • Consider new panels if there’s widespread corrosion or structural issues.

Temporary fix: replace critical fasteners and apply butyl-backed reinforcement at active leak points.

Metal Roof Panel Repairs

Sectional replacement & lap details

Typical Repairs

  • Replace damaged panels; correct panel end-lap distances.
  • Re-seat closures; add foam closures where missing.
  • Seal panel penetrations (solar, satellite, additions) correctly.

When to Re-panel

  • Hail/mechanical damage across large areas.
  • Severe oil-canning or misalignment causing chronic leaks.

SPF (Spray Polyurethane Foam) Roof Repairs

Foam scarification & topcoat

Typical Repairs

  • Scarify (remove) damaged foam, re-foam, shape for drainage.
  • Recoat with silicone/acrylic where topcoat is thin or worn.
  • Seal penetrations, scuppers, and parapet transitions.

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Topcoat worn through from UV; yellow foam showing.
  • Cracks where foam transitions meet equipment curbs.

Repair vs. Replace

  • Repairs are effective when foam is dry and structurally sound.
  • If saturation is present, cut-out and replacement are necessary.

Temporary fix: compatible coating patches to protect exposed foam until full repairs.

Roof Repair FAQs

How fast can you stop an active leak?
During rain, we can often provide emergency dry-in (tarping or temporary patch) the same day. Permanent repairs follow when it’s dry and safe to work.
Do repairs come with a warranty?
Yes—repairs include a workmanship warranty for the specific repair area. If we see signs that a larger replacement is the smarter move, we’ll tell you up-front.
Will you match my shingles or tiles?
We source the closest available match. Weathering and discontinued lines can create slight color differences; we’ll show you options before work begins.
Do small repairs extend the life of a very old roof?
Repairs can buy time, but for systems around ~25+ years in SoCal, recurring repairs can outspend a planned replacement. We’ll help you weigh both paths.
