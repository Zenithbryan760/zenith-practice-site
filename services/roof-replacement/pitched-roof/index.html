Pitched Roof Replacement

Have a sloped roof? This guide explains how pitched roofs work as water-shedding systems, typical slope ranges, and your best replacement options: asphalt shingles (also called architectural / 30-year / three-tab / composition), clay & concrete tile (incl. lightweight), slate, and stone-coated steel metal.

What is a pitched roof (and “water-shedding”)?

A pitched (sloped) roof relies on gravity to shed water quickly off the surface. These systems are designed to overlap shingles/tiles/panels so water runs downhill and off the edges. That’s different from flat/low-slope roofs, which are essentially water-proofing membranes (e.g., TPO) meant to resist standing water.

Typical roof slope ranges and what they mean
Slope (Rise:Run) Type Notes
< 2:12 Low-slope / Flat Use membranes (e.g., TPO, torch-down). Shingles/tiles are not recommended.
2:12 – 4:12 Low pitched Shingles possible with special underlayments; metal panels often ideal.
>= 4:12 Standard pitched Most materials work well: shingles, tile, slate, stone-coated steel.

Best replacement systems for pitched roofs

Asphalt shingles (a.k.a. architectural / 30-year / three-tab / composition)

The most common pitched roof choice. Many names refer to the same general category: asphalt shingles, also called architectural shingles, 30-year shingles, 50-year shingles, three-tab shingles, and composition shingles. The differences are profile and warranty grade.

  • Architectural / dimensional: thicker, layered look, better wind resistance.
  • Three-tab: thinner, flat appearance, budget-friendly.
  • Designer / premium: high-profile, longest warranties, upscale look.

See Asphalt Shingles

Clay & Concrete Tile (incl. lightweight)

A premium, long-lasting option for pitched roofs, popular in Mediterranean and Spanish styles. Concrete tile also comes in lightweight versions to reduce structural load.

  • Excellent durability and curb appeal; heavier than shingles—structure must be rated.
  • Consider Tile Lift & Reset if tiles are reusable but underlayment is aged.

See Tile Roofing

Slate

Natural stone with iconic look and long life when installed correctly. It’s heavy and requires a skilled crew; synthetic slate alternatives can reduce weight/cost.

Stone-Coated Steel & Other Metal

Metal options for pitched roofs include stone-coated steel (looks like shake/tile) and standing-seam metal. They perform well on lower pitches (down to ~3:12 per spec) and shed water quickly.

  • Durable, high wind ratings, excellent water shedding.
  • Lighter than tile; quieter with proper underlayment and attic insulation.

When to replace vs. repair

  • Replace when there’s widespread shingle granule loss, curling, many broken tiles, repeated leaks, or aging underlayment on tile roofs.
  • Repair works for isolated damage, minor flashing issues, or small leak areas.
