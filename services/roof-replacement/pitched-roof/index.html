What counts as a pitched roof?

A pitched (sloped) roof is a water-shedding system — gravity moves water off quickly. If your roof has noticeable slope, you’re in this category (not low-slope membranes like TPO/PVC/torch-down).

  • ✓ Designed to shed water (not hold it)
  • ✓ Uses shingles, tiles, slate, or metal panels
  • ✓ Requires correct underlayment, flashing & ventilation

Common pitched materials

  • Architectural Asphalt Shingles — also called “composition,” “30-year/50-year,” “three-tab” (older) or just “architectural.” Great value and curb appeal.
  • Clay & Concrete Tile — premium look, long-service life; lightweight options available.
  • Steel-Coated Metal — stone-coated panels & standing seam; modern look, high wind/fire performance.
  • Slate — natural or synthetic; beautiful, heavy, and long-lasting.

System-specific notes

Asphalt Shingles

  • ✓ Synthetic underlayment + ice/valley protection as needed
  • ✓ Architectural shingles standard; designer upgrades optional
  • ✓ Ridge ventilation & intake balancing for cooler attic

Tile (Clay/Concrete)

  • ✓ Lift & relay or full replacement depending on underlayment
  • ✓ Lightweight options available after structural check
  • ✓ Bird-stops, head-laps, and valley metal done right

Steel-Coated Metal

  • ✓ Panel layout for wind, expansion, and water management
  • ✓ Hidden fasteners & high-temp underlayment where specified
  • ✓ Excellent fire/wind ratings; modern curb appeal

Slate

  • ✓ Deck review, correct fasteners, flashing craftsmanship
  • ✓ Synthetic slate available where weight or budget demands
