Pitched Roof Replacement

Pitched roofs are water-shedding systems—slope and overlapping layers move water off the home. We replace shingles, tile, metal, and slate with the right underlayment, flashings, and ventilation so your new roof lasts. What Counts as a “Pitched” Roof?

Any roof with enough slope to shed water without ponding—typically 3:12 pitch or steeper. Common materials: asphalt shingles, clay/concrete tile, metal (stone-coated steel or standing seam), and natural slate.

Shingles (Most Common)

Also called: architectural shingles, composition shingles, asphalt shingles, 3-tab shingles, 30-year / 50-year shingles.

Our Process

  1. Contact & scheduling. Call/text or use the form for a free homeowner estimate.
  2. Inspection & leak sourcing. Decking, underlayment, penetrations, flashings, valleys, and ventilation.
  3. Options & written scope. Materials, warranties, ventilation upgrades, and wood repairs as needed.
  4. Prep & protection. Safety lines, property/landscape protection, and staging.
  5. Tear-off & deck repairs. Remove old roof; replace damaged sheathing; re-nail to code.
  6. Underlayment & flashings. Synthetic base; self-sealing membranes at valleys/eaves; new metal flashings.
  7. System install. Installed to manufacturer specs for warranty compliance.
  8. Quality check & cleanup. Detail inspection, magnet sweep, and photo documentation.

General Process by System

Asphalt / Composition Shingles

  • Best for: most homes; cost-effective; many styles/colors.
  • Tear-off & deck work: remove shingles; replace bad sheathing; re-nail deck.
  • Underlayment: synthetic base; self-sealing membrane at valleys/eaves/penetrations as required.
  • Flashings: new drip edge, pipe boots, step & counter-flashing, chimney/skylight flashings.
  • Install: architectural (laminated) or 3-tab; correct nailing; ridge caps; balanced ventilation.

Shingle options & styles

Clay & Concrete Tile (incl. Lightweight)

  • Best for: longevity and curb appeal (Mediterranean/Spanish profiles).
  • Lift & reset or tear-off: save good tiles, replace waterproofing/underlayment, re-install tiles.
  • Underlayment: high-performance tile underlayment; valley metal; battens where specified.
  • Flashings: headwall/sidewall/chimney/skylight; bird-stops and closures as needed.
  • Install: replace broken tiles; proper fastening per profile and wind zone.

Tile systems overview · Tile Lift & Reset

Metal (Stone-Coated Steel & Standing Seam)

  • Best for: durability, energy efficiency, lighter weight.
  • Deck & underlayment: confirm fastening substrate; high-temp underlayment where needed.
  • Flashings: custom trim at eaves/rakes/valleys/ridges; specialized pipe/vent flashings.
  • Install: interlocking panels or concealed-fastener seams; expansion/clip details; hemmed edges.

Metal roofing info

Slate

  • Best for: premium look and very long service life.
  • Deck & flashings: solid decking; copper/stainless flashings and fasteners.
  • Install: slate layout & coursing; proper headlap; specialty ridge/hip details.

Repair or Full Replacement?

If damage is isolated, a focused repair may be right. If you see widespread wear (brittle shingles, curling tiles, repeated leaks, exhausted underlayment), a full replacement protects your home and can lower long-term costs.
