What is a Pitched Roof?

A pitched roof is a water-shedding system—rain flows down and off the roof by gravity. This is different from flat/low-slope roofs, which are water-proofing systems designed to hold water briefly. If your roof has visible slope, hips, or valleys, it’s likely pitched.

1) Free Homeowner Estimate

Call, text, or book online. We confirm roof type, slope, access, and your goals (budget, curb appeal, warranty).

If leaks are active, we source the leak, dry-in, and plan replacement phases around weather windows.

FAQ

Are “architectural,” “30-year,” “50-year,” and “composition” shingles the same thing?

Homeowners use those terms interchangeably. Today’s mainstream choice is the architectural (laminated) composition shingle. Warranty lengths vary by brand/line; we’ll show good/better/best options during your estimate.

Can I switch from tile to shingles (or vice versa)?

Usually, yes—but we verify structure, weight, HOA, and local code. We’ll recommend the safest path and price both options if you’re undecided.

How long does a pitched roof replacement take?

Typical single-family homes take a few days, weather-dependent. Complex roofs, tile/slate resets, and structural or decking repairs can add time—we’ll outline a realistic schedule in your proposal.

Is the underlayment really that important?

Yes. On pitched systems the shingles/tile shed water; the underlayment is your continuous waterproofing layer. Valleys and penetrations also rely on proper metal flashings. We treat those details like the system’s backbone.
