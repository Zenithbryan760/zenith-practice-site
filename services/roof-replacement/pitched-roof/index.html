Pitched Roof Replacement

Have a sloped roof? Here are the most common replacement systems, when each makes sense, and how to choose the right one for your home.

Best Options for Pitched Roofs

Asphalt Shingles

Most popular, wide color choices, good value.

  • Good for most pitched roofs (4:12+ ideal; as low as 2:12 with underlayment upgrades).
  • Varies by grade (3-tab, architectural, designer).

See Asphalt Shingles

Clay & Concrete Tile

Durable, premium look, heavy.

  • Requires structure rated for tile weight.
  • Great for Mediterranean and Spanish styles.

See Tile Roofing

Tile Lift & Reset

Reuse existing tiles with new underlayment.

  • Good when tiles are in good shape.
  • Refreshes waterproofing layer.

See Tile Lift & Reset

Can I switch materials?

  • Shingles → Tile: Maybe. We must verify framing and sheathing for weight and check local code.
  • Tile → Shingles: Usually yes. Lightens the load, may need deck repairs where battens sat.
  • Any pitched roof → Coatings? Typically no. Coatings are for flat/low-slope systems.

When to replace vs. repair

  • Widespread granule loss, curling, broken tiles, or multiple leaks? Replacement is more cost-effective.
  • Isolated leak or small damaged area? Targeted repair may be fine.
