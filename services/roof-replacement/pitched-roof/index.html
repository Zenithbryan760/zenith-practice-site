What Is a Pitched Roof?

A pitched roof uses gravity to shed water—steeper slopes, hips, valleys, and ridges move rain quickly off the home. That’s different from low-slope/flat roofs (which are water-proofing systems). If you can see a visible slope from the ground, you likely have a pitched roof.

Our Process

1) Free Homeowner Estimate

Call, text, or book online. We confirm roof type, slope, access, and your goals (budget, appearance, warranty).

2) Leak Source & Scope

If active leaks exist, we dry-in as needed and plan your replacement around weather windows.

3) Options & Proposal

Clear good/better/best choices with materials, warranties, and timelines—so you pick what fits best.

4) Tear-Off & Deck Prep

Remove roofing, inspect sheathing, replace damaged decking, set ventilation paths per code.

5) Waterproofing & Flashing

Install underlayment/ice-water barriers as required; new valleys, step/headwall flashings, and penetrations.

6) Installation & QA

System install, edge metals, ridge/hip finish, full cleanup, and final photo report.

FAQ

Are “architectural,” “composition,” and “30/50-year” shingles the same?

Homeowners use those terms interchangeably. The modern standard is the architectural (laminated) composition shingle. Warranty years vary by brand/line.

Can I switch from tile to shingles (or the other way)?

Usually yes—structure, weight, HOA, and local code must be checked first. We’ll verify and price out viable options.

How long does replacement take?

Most single-family pitched roofs take a few days, weather-dependent. Tile/slate resets or decking repairs can add time—we’ll outline a realistic schedule.

What matters most for longevity?

Details: underlayment quality, correct flashing in valleys/walls/penetrations, and balanced ventilation. We build to spec so your roof lasts.
