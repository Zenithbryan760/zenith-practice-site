What counts as a pitched roof?

A pitched (sloped) roof is a water-shedding system — gravity moves water off the surface. If you can see a noticeable slope (about 3:12 or steeper), you’re in this category. These differ from low-slope/flat systems that rely on waterproof membranes.

  • ✓Designed to shed water (not hold it)
  • ✓Uses shingles, tiles, slate, or metal panels
  • ✓Needs proper ventilation, underlayment & flashings
