What counts as a “pitched” roof?

A pitched (sloped) roof is a water-shedding roof—gravity moves water off the surface quickly. If your roof has noticeable slope (≈ 3:12 and up), you’re almost certainly in the pitched category.

Is asphalt shingle the same as architectural/composition/three-tab?
Yes — homeowners use different names. We’ll match the right shingle profile and warranty level for your budget and neighborhood.
Can I switch materials (e.g., tile → shingles or shingles → metal)?
Often yes, but we’ll verify structure, weight, ventilation, local code, and HOA (if applicable) before recommending a change.
How long does a pitched roof replacement take?
Most homes are 1–4 days depending on size, layers, decking repairs, and weather.
Do you handle permits and clean-up?
Yes. We pull permits as required, protect landscaping, magnet sweep for nails, and haul away debris.
