What counts as a pitched roof?

A pitched (sloped) roof is a water-shedding system—gravity moves water off the surface quickly. If your roof has noticeable slope (for example 3:12 and up), you’re almost certainly in the pitched category. Pitched systems are different from low-slope/flat roofs (water-proofing membranes like TPO/PVC/torch-down).

  • ✓ Designed to shed water, not hold it
  • ✓ Uses shingles, tiles, slate, or metal panels
  • ✓ Requires proper ventilation, flashing, and underlayment

Common pitched roof materials

  • Most popular + cost-effective; great curb appeal and warranties
  • Wide color selection, impact/wind ratings, algae-resistant options

Clay & Concrete Tile (incl. lightweight)

  • Premium look, long service life, excellent in SoCal sun
  • Lightweight options available for certain structures

Steel-Coated Metal (stone-coated panels & standing seam)

  • Durable, energy-efficient, class-leading wind & fire ratings
  • Profiles that mimic shake/tile or sleek standing-seam modern

Slate (natural or engineered)

  • Timeless, high-end appearance; long lifespan
  • Requires experienced installers & proper structural review

Pitched Roof Replacement — FAQs

How long does a replacement take?

Most homes wrap in 1–3 days for shingles, 2–5 days for tile/metal/slate depending on size and details.

Do you help me choose the right system?

Yes. We compare look, budget, lifespan, energy, and maintenance to land on the best fit for your home.

Will you check ventilation and flashing?

Absolutely—proper intake/exhaust and leak-proof flashing are part of every replacement.

Do you offer repairs instead of replacement?

Yes—if a repair makes sense, we’ll recommend it. See Shingle Repair.
