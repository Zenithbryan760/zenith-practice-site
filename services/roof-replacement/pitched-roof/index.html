Pitched Roof Replacement

Pitched roofs are water-shedding systems—they rely on slope and overlapping layers to move water off the home. We replace shingles, tile, metal, and slate roofs with the right underlayment, flashings, and ventilation so your new system lasts. What Counts as a “Pitched” Roof?

Any roof with enough slope to shed water without ponding—typically 3:12 pitch or steeper. Materials include asphalt shingles, clay/concrete tile, metal systems (stone-coated steel, standing seam), and natural slate.

Our Process

  1. Contact & scheduling. Call/text or use the form for a free homeowner estimate.
  2. Inspection & leak sourcing. We check decking, underlayment, penetrations, flashings, valleys, and ventilation.
  3. Options & written scope. We outline materials, warranties, ventilation upgrades, and any wood repairs.
  4. Prep & protection. Safety lines, landscape protection, property protection, and staging.
  5. Tear-off & deck repairs. Remove old roof, replace bad sheathing, re-nail to code.
  6. Underlayment & flashings. Synthetic base, ice- & water-style membranes in valleys/eaves (as required), new metal flashings.
  7. New roof install. System-specific installation to manufacturer specs for warranty compliance.
  8. Quality check & cleanup. Detail inspection, magnet sweep for nails, final walkthrough with photos.

General Process by System

Asphalt/Composition Shingles

  • Best for: most homes; cost-effective; broad color/styles.
  • Tear-off & deck work: remove existing shingles; replace damaged sheathing; re-nail deck.
  • Underlayment: synthetic base; self-sealing membrane at valleys/eaves/penetrations as required.
  • Flashings: new drip edge, pipe boots, step & counter-flashing, chimney/skylight flashings.
  • Install: architectural (laminated) shingles or 3-tab; correct nailing pattern; ridge caps; balanced ventilation.

Shingle options & styles

Clay & Concrete Tile (incl. Lightweight)

  • Best for: longevity and curb appeal in Mediterranean/Spanish styles.
  • Tear-off/lift & reset: remove tiles (save good tiles), replace waterproofing/underlayment, re-install tiles.
  • Underlayment: high-performance tile underlayment; valley metal; batten systems where specified.
  • Flashings: headwall/sidewall/chimney/skylight flashings; bird-stops and closures as needed.
  • Install: re-use matching tiles where possible; replace broken tiles; proper fastening per profile & wind zone.

Tile systems overview · Tile Lift & Reset

Metal (Stone-Coated Steel & Standing Seam)

  • Best for: durability, energy efficiency, and low weight.
  • Deck & underlayment: confirm fastening substrate; install high-temp underlayment where needed.
  • Flashings: custom metal trim at eaves, rakes, valleys, ridges; specialized pipe/vent flashings.
  • Install: interlocking panels or concealed-fastener seams; attention to expansion, clips, and hems.

Metal roofing info

Slate

  • Best for: premium aesthetics and very long service life.
  • Deck & flashings: solid decking; copper/stainless flashings and fasteners for longevity.
  • Install: slate layout & coursing; proper headlap; specialty ridge/hip details.

Repair or Full Replacement?

If damage is isolated, a focused repair may be the right move. If you see widespread wear (brittle shingles, curling tiles, repeated leaks, bad underlayment), a full replacement protects your home and may reduce long-term costs.
