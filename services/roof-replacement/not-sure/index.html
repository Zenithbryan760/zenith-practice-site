Find My Roof Type

Answer two quick checks to identify your roof. Then see which systems fit and which don’t.

Step 1 — Slope (Pitched vs. Flat/Low-Slope)

  • Pitched/Sloped: You clearly see a slope from the street. Common surfaces: shingles (small overlapping rectangles) or tile (curved “S” or flat tiles).
  • Flat / Low-Slope: Looks flat or barely sloped. Surfaces often look smooth, light-colored sheets (TPO/PVC) or dark mineral surface (torch down).

Can I put that roof on my house?

  • Shingles on a flat roof? No — shingles need slope. Use TPO or torch down instead.
  • Tile instead of shingles? Maybe — tile is heavier. We check structure, sheathing, and local code first.
  • Coatings instead of replacement? Sometimes — silicone coatings can extend life on certain flat roofs after prep.

Things that can change the answer

  • HOA/City rules: color and material limits may apply.
  • Framing & weight: tile needs structure rated for it.
  • Existing layers: tear-off requirements can apply.
  • Drainage & flashing: flat areas need correct slope and scuppers.
